PLANETARY SCIENCE · LUNAR GEOLOGY

Adriana Ariza-Pardo

PhD candidate in Planetary Sciences, exploring volcanism and the geology of the Moon and Mars to support future missions under NASA's Artemis program.

01 · ABOUT

I am a geoscientist and PhD candidate in Planetary Sciences at the Università “G. d’Annunzio” (Chieti–Pescara, Italy). My work focuses on the geologic mapping of the Moon and the study of lava flows as analogs to better understand volcanic evolution on other worlds.

I collaborate with international teams preparing for human exploration under the Artemis missions, integrating remote sensing data, laboratory experiments, and terrestrial analog studies.

02 · RESEARCH LINES

Current

Lunar mare margins & Artemis: High-resolution geological mapping to evaluate volcanic history and in-situ resource potential (ISRU) in key Artemis regions.

Extensive lava flows on Mars: Use of analogs and thermal properties to understand how Martian lava flows can reach hundreds of kilometers in length.
